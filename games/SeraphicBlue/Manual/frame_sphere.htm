Frame & Sphere

◆Frame Explanation
A frame is basically a body armor that a character wears.
It is like a barrier that protects a character and helps in reducing damage against enemy attacks.


◆Sphere Explanation
A sphere is a ball-shaped accessory equipment that is equipped to a character's frame, depending on how many sphere slots the frame contains.
Spheres in Seraphic Blue have many varieties, such as unlocking access to magic abilities, providing elemental and debuff boosts and resistances to physical attacks and giving stat boosts to party members.
A character can only utilise a sphere's effects if it is equipped to a character's frame by accessing the Arms section of the main menu.


Frame and Sphere Equipment System

In a normal character equipment system, a character's equipments are simply categorised into different types (Weapon, Armor, Accessory, Gloves, Boots etc) as depicted in the diagram on the left of the picture below.
In a frame and sphere character equipment system however, it is very different from a normal character equipment system.
Instead of many different categories of equipments, this system only has two main categories of equipments: Weapon & Frame. The frame is further categorised into many sphere categories, indicating that the spheres are equipped to a frame and that without the frame, spheres cannot be equipped. This is as depicted in the diagram on the right of the picture below.

The number of sphere slots in a frame is indicated by SPC (Sphere Capacity) when you equip a frame to a character.
As the game progresses, players will be able to buy higher-level frames that contain more sphere slots, and with more sphere slots, players will have more freedom and options in selecting the best spheres for a character.


Types of Frames

There are 4 main types of Frames, namely Mail, Mesh, Robe and Cross.

Mail A mail offers great DEF but poor MDF. It is a heavy frame that can only be equipped by warriors (heavyweight characters).
Mesh A mesh offers decent DEF and MDF.
It is lighter than a mail, and can be equipped by all characters except mages.
Robe A robe offers low DEF but great MDF.
It is highly sought after by mages, and that only mages can equip robes.
Most robes have higher SPC than other frames.
Cross A cross offers decent DEF and MDF but is weaker than a mesh.
It has no special characteristics whatsoever, and any character can equip a cross. It is also the cheapest of all the frames.



Types of Spheres

There are 4 main types of Spheres, namely Ability, Invest, Tactical and Guard Spheres.

Ability Sphere
（Ａ-Sphere）		 Ability Spheres unlock access to magic abiilties. They range from levels 1 to 6.
For example, a 『Curative Magic Level 3』 ability sphere unlocks access to Level 1 to Level 3 curative magic spells.
Characters cannot use any specific magic abilities without first equipping the specific ability sphere.
Invest Sphere
（Ｉ-Sphere）		 Invest Spheres focus on elements and negative status effects (aka debuffs).
These spheres boost elemental offense and defense strengths, and debuffs' inflict and resistance rates.
There are also field element-altering Invest Spheres that enables characters to produce a specific field element into battle via physical attack/ability, eg. equipping a water-field element Invest sphere on a character will allow him/her to produce the water element into the battle field.

For example, if a 『Fire Offense: 20』 sphere is equipped, it raises a character's fire offense strength by 20 points.
If a 『Poison Resist: 20%』 sphere is equipped、it raises a character's resistance to poison rate by 20%.

Invest spheres are especially very useful in countering against monsters that specialise on debuffs or have very strong elemental attacks that can kill your entire party instantly etc.

Tactical Sphere
（Ｔ-Sphere）		 Tactical spheres provide special enhancements and passive abilities in battle.
Examples of tactical spheres include Magic Damage 1.5x, Counter Attack, Auto-Defense etc.
Guard Sphere
（Ｇ-Sphere）		 Guard spheres are miscellaneous equipments (other than weapon & frame) that provide additional stat boosts to characters.
The following shows the various types of Guard spheres:

Shields & Walls These equipments boost DEF. Shields can only be equipped by heavyweight warriors, while walls can be equipped by all other characters.
However, walls gradually become weaker with more battles.
Greaves & Boots Equipments worn at the feet. These equipments boost DEF, AGL & MOV, and also raises resistance to Slow. Greaves can only be equipped by heavyweight warriors, while boots can be equipped by all other characters.
Helms & Hats Equipments worn at the head. These equipments bosst DEF & MDF, and also raises resistances to Blind, Forget, Confuse & Melancholy.
Helms can only be equipped by heavyweight warriors, while hats can be equipped by all other characters.
Gloves Equipments worn at the hands. These equipments boost ATK, HIT, CRT & DEF.
All characters except mages can equip gloves.
Accessory Any other equipments that are not any of the above-mentioned are accessories. Accessories provide special boost effects to characters..



Endless Possibilities of Sphere Combinations

As players gain access to more sphere slots per character, players have more freedom to make the characters stronger.
Players can utilise the best out of the characters with the maximum of 6 sphere slots provided by the highest level normal frame (though there are rare frames that provide more than 6 sphere slots; every character can have a possibility of up to 8 sphere slots).
There are no right or wrong spheres to equip to a character. Players have the freedom of trying many different possible sphere combinations for battles, so it is up to the players to figure out what works best for them in different situations.
