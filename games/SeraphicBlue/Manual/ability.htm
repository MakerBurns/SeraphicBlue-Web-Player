Ability Explanation

Abilities are special skills used by characters in battle and cost TP for usage.
While most abilities can only be used in battles, some abilities like HP and TP healing abilities can be used outside of battles as well (via the Ability menu).

Types of Abilities

There are 2 main categories of abilities:

Physical Abilities Physical abilities deal with physical attacks. All physical abilities are disabled if a character is inflicted with Blind.
The amount of damage dealt by physical abilities is determined by a character's ATK stat.
・Personal Abilities (physical)
Magical Abilities Magical abilities deal with magical attacks. All magical abilities are disabled if a character is inflicted with Forget.
The amount of damage dealt by magical abilities is determined by a character's MEN stat.

Magic Explanation

In order for a character to use magic, players have to buy Ability Spheres from shops. They are labelled as, eg. Level 1 Attack Magic.
The Ability Spheres range from levels 1 to 6, and each character has a certain limit to the level of a magic Sphere that he/she can equip.
Most warrior characters have lower Ability Sphere level limits than most mage characters.

Below are the 3 main types of magic:

Attack Magic Equip an Attack Magic Sphere to unlock offensive magic spells. These spells do purely damage on enemies.
The effectiveness of the damage dealt by offensive magic spell is determined by a character's MEN stat.
Curative Magic Equip a Curative. Magic Sphere to unlock curative magic spells. These spells mainly heal and resurrect allies.
The effectiveness of the healing powers of curative magic is determined by a character's MEN stat.
Support Magic   Equip a Support. Magic Sphere to unlock supportive magic spells. These spells mainly debuff enemies and buff allies. The success rate of casting supportive magic spells is 100%, and the success rate of debuffs on enemies depends on the enemies' resistances to negative status effects.

There is also the existence of Ability Spheres beyond Level 6, known as the <Ultimate Spells>. Players can only obtain them under special circumstances. Only characters who have the capability to learn the maximum Level 6 magic abilities can have the privilege to learn the Ultimate Spells.

Personal Abilities Explanation

Personal abilities are abilities that are unique to each character in Seraphic Blue.
Characters learn new personal abilities either via levelling up or through in-game events.
Various types of personal abilities include Physical, Magical & Support personal abilities.


AR／AE／AC／AWT

The acronyms used in the ability description (as shown in the picture) are AR, AE, AC and AWT.
These are especially important information for players when in battle. Below are their descriptions:

AR(Ability Range) Indicates the type of target range that the ability can cover on enemies/allies. Examples of these types include single, all and self-targets. A Single/All Target ability can apply to both a single target and all targets. (To switch between single and all targets, press Up and Down while selecting enemy targets and Left and Right while selecting ally targets)
AE(Ability Element) Indicates the element that an ability is imbued in. Using an elemental ability will cast the ability's element onto the field in battle.
AC(Ability Component) Indicates the field element in battle that an ability requires in order to cast it.

AWT(Ability Waiting Time)

(Not to be confused with ATKWT - Attack Waiting Time)

Indicates the amount of time that a character has to wait for his/her next turn after casting the ability.
Note the difference in how the AWT works between physical and magical abilities below:

・Physical Ability：
A physical ability's AWT is indicated by eg. 「+150」, where there is a + sign indicated in front of the number.
This means the AWT here is not the true AWT. It is added to a character's ATKWT to indicate the final AWT, eg. A character's ATKWT is 500, hence by using an ability with an AWT of +150, the final AWT of the ability is 650.

・Magical Ability：
A magical ability's AWT is indicated by eg. 「650」, where there is no + sign indicated in front of the number.
In this case, the AWT here is the true AWT and nothing else is added to this AWT.
