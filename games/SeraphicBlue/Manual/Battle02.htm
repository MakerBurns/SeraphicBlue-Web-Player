◆Information on Battle Elements & Status Effects

The 8 Elements

There are 8 elements in the world of Seraphic Blue, namely 「Fire／Water／Wind／Earth／Light／Dark／Sky／Break」.
Fire conflicts with Water, Wind conflicts with Earth and Light conflicts with Dark. Only Sky and Break have no opposing elements.

Fire⇔Water
Wind⇔Earth
Light⇔Dark
Sky⇔No Opposing Element
Break⇔No Opposing Element

These are the above elements' acronyms that are used in-game: Fire - Fire, Water - Wtr, Wind - Wind, Earth - Eth, Light - Lgt, Dark - Dark, Sky - Sky and Break - Brk.

Brief explanations of the Sky and Break elements: The Sky element relates to attacks that are in relation to the sky, eg. lightning attacks. The Break element relates to attacks that cause the 'break' effect and deliver a huge repulsive impact on enemies, eg. shockwave attacks.

Altering Elemental Damage ／Elemental Attack & Defense

Attack damage isn't just affected by a character's ATK & DEF stats. It is also affected by a character's elemental attack and defenses if the attack has an element.
Below shows how elemental attack and defense work when you equip an elemental invest sphere to a character:

Attack Equip an elemental attack invest sphere to increase a respective element's attack damage. The elemental attack damage range is from 0 to 100.
・Fire ATK 100 → Your character's fire-elemental attack damage is maxed.
・Fire ATK 50 → Your character's fire-elemental attack damage increases by a decent amount.
・Fire ATK 20 → Your character's fire-elemental attack damage increases by a little.
Hence an elemental attack is strongest at ATK 100.
Defense Equip an elemental defense invest sphere to increase a character's defense to a respeective element's attack damage.
Unlike elemental attack, the elemental defense range is from 0 to 200, and all characters start from 100.
・Fire DEF 200 → Your character is strongest to fire.
・Fire DEF 150 → Your character is strong against fire.
・Fire DEF 120 → Your character is slightly strong against fire.
・Fire DEF 100 → Your character is neither weak nor strong against fire.
・Fire DEF 80 → Your character is slightly weak against fire.
・Fire DEF 50 → Your character is weak against fire.
・Fire DEF 0 → Your character is weakest to fire.

Note that elemental attack and defense co-relate to each other, so the final elemental damage is dependant on both factors.
Below shows 2 scenarios of how damage is calculated based on elemental attack and defense:

Eg. １：
An attack that deals 1000 damage by default without any elemental assistance is now supported by a Fire ATK 100 invest sphere.

Fire DEF 200 ： Character completely resists against fire and no damage is dealt.
Fire DEF 150 ： Character resists fire damage by a decent amount and the damage is reduced to 500.
Fire DEF 100 ： Character is neither weak nor strong against fire and It's still the same 1000 damage.
Fire DEF 50 ： Character succumbs to fire damage by a decent amount and the damage is increased to 1500.
Fire DEF 0 ： Character becomes completely weak to fire and the damage is increased to the max of 2000 damage.

Eg. ２：
This time, the same attack that deals 1000 damage by default without any elemental assistance is supported by a Fire ATK 50 invest sphere.
Notice that more damage is actually dealt than in Eg. 1 when the DEF is above 100, but it is still lesser than the default 1000 damage. Of course, compared to Eg. 1, lesser damage is dealt when the DEF is below 100.

Fire DEF 200 ： Character completely resists against only half of its full fire ATK strength (50 out of 100) and hence the damage is reduced to 500, which would be the non-fire damage.
Fire DEF 150 ： Character resists fire damage by a decent amount and the damage is reduced to 750.
Fire DEF 100 ： Character is neither weak nor strong against fire and It's still the same 1000 damage.
Fire DEF 50 ： Character succumbs to fire damage by a decent amount and the damage is increased to 1250.
Fire DEF 0 ： Character becomes completely weak to fire and the damage is increased to the max of 1500 damage.

In summary,

・If the Element ATK is 100 and/or Element DEF is 200, the element completely comes into play in the attack damage.
・If the Element ATK is 0 and/or Element DEF is 100, the element does not come into play in the attack damage at all and only the normal attack damage is registered.

Note: In cases where an elemental attack invest sphere is not used but an elemental attack ability is used, it will work the same way as Eg. 1, whereby Element DEF 200 would result in no damage while Element DEF 0 would result in the max damage (normal + elemental damage).

Casting Field Elements (FE)

The circle slots located at the bottom right of a battle's enemy screen are known as the field element slots. They represent the respective elements and an element appears in a field element slot when an elemental ability is used in battle.
For example, when there are no field elements in play and a fire ability is used, the fire element appears in a field element slot. This is what is known as casting a field element into the battlefield.

When there are no field elements in play, it means the field element slots are in the neutral state. Each of the 5 slots corresponds to 2 elements that oppose each other (with the exception of Sky and Break elements which have no opposing elements, meaning they occupy the last 2 field elemental slots by themselves).
So, for example, if a fire field element is in play and a water elemental ability is used, the fire will be neutralised and the field element slot hence becomes empty. Below shows the respective order of how the field elements are stacked (top row covers the leftmost field element slot while bottom row covers the rightmost field element slot).

（Fire） ＜Fire⇔Neutral⇔Water＞ （Water）
（Wind） ＜Wind⇔Neutral⇔Earth＞ （Earth）
（Light） ＜Light⇔Neutral⇔Dark＞ （Dark）
（Sky） ＜Sky⇔Neutral＞ （Anti-Sky）
（Break） ＜Break⇔Neutral＞ （Anti-Break）

There are 2 ways to cast field elements into the battlefield:

Physical Attack Equip a FE-casting invest sphere to a character to allow him to cast a FE into the battle field via a physical attack.
Ability Cast an ability that has AE (Ability Element）(as shown in picture below) to cast the respective FE into the battle field.
However, if an ability has both the same AE and AC (Ability Component), it means the ability requires that AC field element in play to cast the ability. Using this ability will cause the field element to be activated and turning to neutral state as a result.



FE & Ability Component（AC）

The type of FE that certain abilities require to be cast in battle are indicated by the abilities' Ability Component (AC).
Most of the FE-required abilities are high-levelled and highly damaging, and so players will have to manage the field elements well in order to utilise the FE-required abilities well to their fullest.
In an example shown in the above screenshots, see that the ability 『Giga Break』's AC is of the Break element, which means Giga Break requires the Break field element to be cast in battle. Since the Break field element is in play in the battlefield as shown in the left screenshot, Giga Break is hence possible to be cast in battle (which is also indicated if the words "Giga Break" are lit up and not gray in color). As such, Giga Break is cast, dealing tremendous damage to the enemy. At the same time, the Break field element is activated and turns to neutral state as a result, as shown in the right screenshot.

因みにACに２つの属性がオア（or）条件で設定されている場合、何れか片方のFEが在れば発動が可能です。
両方とも在る場合は、ランダムで何れか片方のFEが使用されます。

※新次元の攻防／高度アビリティの発動と阻止
ACを使用する高度アビリティは概して非常に強力です。
効率的にFEを準備してこれらのアビリティをコンスタントに発動出来る様なバトル運びを実現すれば、
勝利はより確実な物になるでしょう。

しかしながら、これらのアビリティを発動するのは敵もまた同じ事です。
その猛烈な威力が逆に自分達の身に襲い掛かり、戦況の悪化が避けられないばかりか、
最悪の場合は一撃全滅という結末を迎えるかも知れません。

味方の高度アビリティ発動をサポートし、敵の高度アビリティ発動を阻止するという、新次元の攻防が展開されます。
特に数々の強敵が待ち受けるゲーム後半以降は、この攻防を制しなければ勝利は得られないでしょう。


Positive & Negative Status Effects

バトル中、ユニットは様々な状態に移行しますが、
この内不利に働く物を不利状態、有利に働く物を有利状態と呼びます。
バトルに勝利するにはこれら不利状態の防止や回復と、有利状態の有効活用が必要不可欠と言えるでしょう。

Negative Status Effects （10 in Total）

D(Dead) A character is dead if his/her HP is 0. When revived, characters will begin from the maximum WT of 900.
S(Stone) When a character is stoned, he/she stops moving in the WT gauge and disappears from it, signifying that he/she has 'fallen' from battle. When unstoned, characters will reappear in the WT gauge and resume from their last WT before they were stoned.
P(Poison) When poisoned, characters will lose 20% of his/her max HP every turn if unhealed.
D(Disease) Disease works like poison, except that instead of just dealing HPMAX 20% damage, it also removes all positive status effects on a character and decreases his/her ATK and DEF stats. Also, characters cannot be buffed while in Disease state.
P(Paralyse) When paralysed, characters cannot move and will stay fixed in their current WT in the WT gauge. Characters' AVD stat is also decreased to zero when paralysed.
All countermeasure moves like Counter Attack, Advanced Guard and Last-Ditch Attack are disabled as well. in paralysis.
S(Slow) Decreases characters' AGI and hence slows down characters' movement in the WT gauge.
However, the Slow state is neutralised when a character is buffed with the Quick status.
B(Blind) When blinded, characters' HIT rate is decreased and all physical abilities are disabled.
F(Forget) Causes characters to forget all their magic spells. MEN and MDF are decreased when inflicted with Forget and all magical abilities are disabled.
C(Confuse) Confuses characters' senses, making them regard their allies as enemies instead. When in Confuse status, characters are uncontrollable and can only either Normal Attack or Hard Attack their own allies or themselves.
Characters snap out of their Confuse status when they are hit by physical attacks.
M(Melancholy) Inflicts characters with the state of depression. Being the worst status of all, it decreases a character's ATK, DEF, MEN, MDF, AGL and AVD stats. All positive status effects on characters are also removed, and characters cannot gain any positive status effects while in Melancholy status.

Positive Status Effects （4 in Total）

P(Protect) Increase DEF
C(Clever) Increase MEN & MDF
Q(Quick) Increase AGI
F(Force) Increase ATK

・戦闘不能と石化の場合は戦闘離脱扱いとしてWTゲージからアイコンが消えます。
味方全員がこの状態に陥ると全滅です。

・戦闘不能と石化以外は、放っておいても時間が経てば回復します。
回復する確率はランダムで、時間が経てば経つ程高くなります。

・毒と病気のダメージに関して、他のRPGではHP１からは減少しないといった扱いも在りますが、
このバトルでは戦闘不能に陥りますので注意してください。
例えばHP90/1000の病気状態で行動順になった場合、
行動前に100のダメージを受けて戦闘不能、何も出来ないまま次のユニットの行動に移ります。

・全ての不利状態は戦闘が終了すると自動的に解除されます。
戦闘不能の場合はHP１の状態で回復します。

・全ての有利状態は一定WTが経過すると解除されます。
